1 "Tell me about a time you had to manage competing priorities across multiple executives simultaneously."

Talking Point: Draw on your current Gen4 role — supporting the CEO while also coordinating across the broader leadership team. Describe a specific scenario: a board meeting week, a leadership summit, or a time when multiple executive needs collided. Walk through how you triaged, communicated, and resolved without dropping anything. Emphasize the systems you use (shared calendars, daily briefings, priority queues) and the proactive communication that prevents conflicts before they happen.

2 "What does 'high emotional intelligence' look like to you in an EA role?"

Talking Point: This question is testing for self-awareness and nuance. Talk about reading a room before scheduling a difficult conversation. Talk about knowing when to interrupt an executive vs. when to shield them. Reference your Barclays experience — investment banking environments are high-stakes and high-pressure; reading the executive's emotional state and energy level was part of the job. EQ also means managing up with grace: knowing how to give feedback or push back diplomatically when needed.

3 "How have you handled a situation that required extreme confidentiality?"

Talking Point: In financial services (Barclays, UBS), confidentiality is foundational. Reference your experience with sensitive materials — deal information, compensation data, executive personnel matters, or board-level communications. You don't need to name specifics; what matters is demonstrating that you understand why confidentiality is non-negotiable and that you've built habits around it (secure file handling, discretion in conversations, not repeating what you hear). At Gen4, you have similar exposure around executive decisions, personnel, and company strategy.

4 "How do you prepare executive materials for a strategic planning session?"

Talking Point: Walk through your actual process: gathering agenda items and pre-reads from each stakeholder ahead of time, formatting executive briefings to be scannable (not walls of text), managing the technology/logistics (room setup, video conferencing, materials distribution), following up with action items and notes post-meeting. If you've supported quarterly business reviews, annual planning sessions, or board prep at Gen4, describe what that looks like. Specificity wins here — "I send a pre-read packet to all attendees 48 hours before the session" is much stronger than "I help prepare materials."

5 "Why are you leaving Gen4 Dental Partners, and why Inspira Financial?"

Talking Point: Keep it forward-looking and positive. "I've loved my time at Gen4 and learned a great deal about what exceptional executive support looks like at the C-suite level. As I think about the next chapter, I'm drawn back to financial services — where I built my foundational EA skills at Barclays and UBS. Inspira sits at the intersection of finance and employee benefits, which I find genuinely compelling. The role here aligns with my seniority, my industry roots, and my interest in supporting executive leadership with real strategic weight." Avoid any negative framing about Gen4.
