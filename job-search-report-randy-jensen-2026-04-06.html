Describe the scope (which modules, how many users, regulatory environment), your configuration decisions (workflow design, routing rules, role structures), the validation work (IQ/OQ/PQ), and the outcome (FDA inspection readiness, adoption metrics). Emphasize that you had skin in the game as the sysadmin — you weren't just configuring for a customer, you were the quality team's representative. Also mention the SmartSolve migration as a separate project that shows migration and data transition expertise.
2"Describe a situation where a customer's MasterControl configuration was causing compliance risk. How did you handle it?"
Talking Points: Reference the Polarity TE situation — you inherited "an unwieldy MasterControl configuration" with a paper-based document numbering system force-fitted into the platform. Walk through your diagnostic process, how you identified the risks (workflow gaps, numbering inconsistencies), how you restructured without disrupting ongoing operations, and how you aligned the new configuration with 21 CFR 1271/820 best practices. This story also demonstrates your "quality systems manager who lived in the system" perspective, which is a differentiator over pure-vendor PSC candidates. As a PSC, you'll encounter clients with configurations like this regularly — you'll be remediating, not just building.
3"How do you manage a project when a client pushes back on your recommended configuration approach?"
Talking Points: Draw on your TAM experience managing 15–20 enterprise accounts and achieving 98% account retention. Emphasize your client-first consultative approach: you listen to understand the business reason behind the pushback, validate whether it reflects a regulatory requirement, a workflow preference, or a previous bad experience, and then educate rather than mandate. Acknowledge that your years as an end-user give you credibility when explaining why certain configurations hold up to FDA inspections — you can say "I've been in your shoes" honestly. Share a specific scenario if possible where you found a creative middle-ground solution that met the client's operational need while maintaining compliance integrity.
4"What do you know about MasterControl's AQEM platform and how have you worked with it?"
Talking Points: Your resume notes "working knowledge of AQEM, MasterControl's next-generation event management platform." Be specific about what you know: AQEM replaces the legacy Process module for event management (CAPA, deviations, complaints) with a more modern, configurable architecture. If you've had hands-on exposure through your Learning Consultant or consulting roles, describe that. If your knowledge is primarily conceptual, be honest and frame it positively: "I've stayed current with AQEM's roadmap throughout my time at MasterControl and am eager to deepen my hands-on configuration experience — particularly for the growing customer base transitioning from the legacy Process modules." This shows self-awareness and initiative.
5"You left MasterControl in 2025 and are now applying to return. Why?"
Talking Points: Be direct and confident — there's no need to be defensive about this. Frame it as: "I left to test my skills independently, and I've validated that I can own client engagements outside the MasterControl structure. What I've found is that the Professional Services Consultant role aligns better with how I work — deep implementation partnerships, cross-industry exposure, and the ability to contribute to MasterControl's mission rather than being adjacent to it." Emphasize that returning as a PSC is a deliberate choice, not a fallback: the PSC role is a better fit for your skillset than your previous training-focused role, and your post-MasterControl consulting experience has sharpened your implementation instincts. Boomerang employees typically onboard faster and contribute sooner — lean into that.
